Barchan dune collisions in the Bodele Depression (Chad)

Although the dynamics of individual barchan dunes is well understood, their interactions are the subject of ongoing scientific interest and debate. Numerical and analog models have predicted that (1) 'head-on' collisions of barchans can have the appearance of solitary waves (aka solitons); and (2) 'off-center' collisions result in mass transfer between dunes. This website contains a number of animations serving to illustrate the first direct real-world observations of these phenomena, using time series of high resolution optical satellite imagery from the Bodele Depression in northern Chad. There are a total of twenty animations of solitary wave behavior and off-center collisions, which can be viewed by clicking on the hyperlinks on top of this page. Depending on your connection, the animations can take a few minutes to load, as they ~2Mb in size each. A full list of the satellite scenes used can be found here. For further details and discussion, see the Science Brevia manuscript entitled "Solitary wave behavior in barchan dunes observed from space".
