Metamorphic conditions, such as heat and pressure change over time and with increased depth. Therefore, the constituent minerals change in order to remain stable under these new conditions. Fabrics and textures we see in metamorphics become very important because they provide clues to the historical processes that have formed and changed the rock. In this example, we can see the biotite crystals show a strong mineral alignment from left to right which is called a preferred orientation and this is a direct response to the pressure system forming this rock. Another feature we can clearly see is the broad separation into bands, either dark mafic layers made of biotite and sillimanite and lighter colours layers made of quartz and feldspar. This compositional layering combined with the preferred orientation of those phylosilicates gives rise to a schistosity, and the rock is therefore called a schist.
