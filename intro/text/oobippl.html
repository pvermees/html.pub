This photograph shows a bivalve shell fragment. In life bivalves shells are made of aragonite, which is the metastable polymorph of calcite. However, upon death and under surface conditions, the aragonite recrystallises to calcite, in a process that is called neomorphism. This means that the aragonite shell disolves and is replaced by a sparry calcite. Neomorphism almost always results in an increase of grain size.
