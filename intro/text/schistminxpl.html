Look carefully at this photomicrograph in XPL. Six minerals are labeled A to F, and some of their key properties listed below.
  • Mineral A has strong birefringence up to 2nd order red. Extinction is parallel to straight crystal sides.
  • Mineral B has moderate birefringence to 2nd order blue with parallel extinction.
  • Mineral C is isotropic and contains a number of inclusions.
  • Mineral D has low birefringence, 1st order greys and undulose extinction.
  • Mineral E has low birefringence, 1st order greys. Lamellar twinning is often present. This mineral often alters to the clay mineral sericite.
  • Mineral F is opaque in PPL and XPL.

(field of view = 4mm)
