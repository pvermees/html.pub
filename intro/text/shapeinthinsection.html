Shape

The shape of a mineral is governed by its crystal structure and is called its habit. There are several habits a mineral might develop.

If the length of the crystal is longer than its width it is called prismatic (eg. olivine), acicular (eg. tourmaline) or bladed. (eg. kyanite).

If the length is shorter than its width it is described as tabular (eg. feldspar) or platy (eg. biotite).

Finally, if its length is approximately the same as its width we call it equant.
