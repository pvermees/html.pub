This is a non-clastic sedimentary rock, essentially composed of allochemical components such as gastropods, bivalves and brachiopods. Some of these allochems are up to 7 mm in length and form helicoid spirals and are filled with a buff coloured mineral, which is too fine grained to be resolvable with the naked eye. The margins of the gastropods are often a darker brown to black in colour. Some gastropods are filled with a buff coloured and very light brown colour. In thin section such dual infilling of gastropods is termed geopetal structure and is used as a way-up indicator.
