In the centre of this photograph you can again see the square-shaped cross sectional view of this mineral. But also on the right hand side you can see the acicular form as well. Again you can see the symmetrical arrangement of the carbonaceous material forming the Maltese Cross. This mineral is an Al2SiO5 polymorph and is called andalusite. The Maltese Cross is characteristic of a particular variety of andalusite known as chiastolite. You will also see some rather diffuse circular shaped gray patches particularly in the northwest quadrant.
