Sedimentary rocks are formed by the accumulation of detrital grains resulting from the erosion of pre-existing rocks or through biological and/or chemical processes.

Sedimentologists use the terms grains or clasts instead of crystals and phenocrysts.

Descriptions should include:
  • An introduction describing primary and secondary features.
  • Description of the grains.
  • Description of the cement/matrix.
  • Name of the rock.
  • Environment of deposition.
