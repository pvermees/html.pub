This photograph shows an echinoderm plate. Echinoderms or sea urchins often disaggregate upon death. Echinoderm plates are very common in carbonate rocks. They are often square or rectangular in shape and surrounded by a micritic envelope. As with all allochems, you should note the average size of the grains and the percentage of this allochem within the rock.

(field of view = 2.5mm)
