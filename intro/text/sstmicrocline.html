This is not exactly the same image as you have just seen but it does show the two new minerals together at high magnification.

Mineral B (just below the centre of the image) is highly birefrigent in XPL, with 4th and 5th order interference colours. It shows two cleavages and goes in extinction symmetric to those cleavages. It is colourless in PPL but can appear to be cloudy. This mineral often forms the cement between clasts. It is calcite.

The shape is of mineral D (centre of image) is somewhat different because it has undergone abrasion (it is abraded). The mineral has low relief and features 1st order birefringence colours. It has obvious twinning, with two sets of twins visible under rotation. This is the classic tartan twinning of microcline feldspar.
