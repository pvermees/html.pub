Cleavage

Cleavage is the splitting or breaking of a crystal along planar surfaces which are determined by the crystal structure and bond strengths in the mineral.

There can be more than one cleavage in hand-specimen with minerals often displaying one, two or three cleavages.

The quality of cleavage is defined as perfect, strong or poor. Also taken into consideration are the angles two or more cleavages make with each other such as amphibole 120o or pyroxene 90o.

Muscovite is said to have a perfect cleavage in one direction
