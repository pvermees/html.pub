Twinning

The mineral labelled A in the photograph is a feldspar and although the two halves of the mineral look different colours, it is all one crystal. The mineral is displaying twinning which is a common feature in feldspars and many other minerals.

A twinned crystal results from the crystal lattice being orientated in different directions. Different geometric relationships between the lattices produces different styles of twins. This is known as the twin laws.

Often the relationship between the twins is symmetrical and can be either rotational or a reflection symmetry.

Twinning in feldspars is very distinctive and is used as a diagnostic feature to identify the particular feldspar.
