Colour

Look at biotite in PPL, initially it is a light pale-brown colour. This is described as its body-colour

Note that as you turn the stage of the microscope the biotite colour changes to a dark-brown colour. This property is called pleochroism and is due to the path light takes through the mineral.

Some minerals are strongly pleochroic, others are only weakly pleochroic and some are not pleochroic at all.

Other minerals in the field of view are quartz and feldspar. Note they are colourless and are not pleochroic.
