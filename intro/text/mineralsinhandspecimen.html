Physical properties of minerals in hand specimen

If a rock is medium or coarse grained it is possible to name it by identifying the individual minerals using a hand-lens or binocular microscope. Each mineral has its own particular properties which are defined by its crystal structure and composition.

Fine grained rocks are difficult to name in hand-specimen because the crystals are too small to be seen, even with a hand-lens.

Always try and use more than one property to identify a mineral.
  • Colour
  • Transparency
  • Habit
  • Cleavage
  • Streak
  • Hardness
  • Specific Gravity
