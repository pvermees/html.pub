Biotite K(Mg,Fe)3AlSi3O10(OH,F)2

Thin-Section.

Colour. Most commonly "straw" brown but various shades of brown and green

Pleochroism. Strong. Commonly from straw to dark brown

Habit. Platy.

Cleavage. Perfect in 1 direction

Relief. Low

Birefringence. Up to 2nd order reds

Twinning. Occasional

Extinction. Parallel. Biotite twinles (mottling) just before maximum extinction

Distinguishing features. Colour, pleochroism and habit. Biotite often shows pleochroic haloes which resemble cigarette type burns which are produced by radioactive decay of zircon.
