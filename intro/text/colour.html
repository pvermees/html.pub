Colour

One of the first things you notice when looking down a microscope is the colour of the minerals, either in plane polarised light or crossed polarised light. There is a gamut of colours ranging from colourless quartz and feldspars, through the greens of chlorite and amphiboles to, the browns of biotite. There are also completely black minerals called opaques.

Obviously colour is an important factor in the identification of minerals but many minerals have a variable chemical composition which can influence the colour of the mineral. Colour should therefore be used in conjunction with other properties to determine the name of the mineral.
