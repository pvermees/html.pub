In the center of this image is an elongate grain oriented northwest to southeast. It appears to be made up of small subhedral grains of quartz in various stages of extinction.

This is termed polycrystalline quartz and is the result of metamorphic recrystallisation. It is therefeore derived from a different source area to that of the monocrystalline quartz grains on the previous page that were suggested to come from an igneous source.

(field of view = 2mm)
