Look at these images in both PPL and XPL. In the centre of this photograph is a square shaped beautiful euhedral crystal. In the middle of this crystal is a dark elongate patch of carbonaceous material that has a symmetrical shape forming a characteristic Maltese Cross. This mineral has high relief, is colourless in PPL, has low first order grey birefringence colours and generally has an elongate or acicular form. Think of looking along a pencil, then turning it around and looking at it end on. That is what you are doing with this mineral. The next photograph demonstrates this point very effectively.
