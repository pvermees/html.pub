This is a coarse grained, crystalline, intrusive igneous rock. It is mostly composed of felsic minerals and is therefore generally light in colour.

Mineral A is colourless and is not pleochroic. Crystals are subhedral, do not show cleavage, have a low relief and a dusty or turbid appearance due to late stage alteration. In cross-polar light mineral A shows weak birefringence to 1st order greys. It shows lamellar twinning, oscillatory zoning, oblique extinction and a Michel Levy angle of about 18o
