Duties and Responsibilities

The Department of Earth Sciences at UCL seeks to appoint a Senior Research Software Engineer to develop geochronological research software.



The project is funded by the Natural Environment Research Council (NERC) for three years in the first instance. Its aim is to implement new algorithms to determine the age of rocks and minerals using isotopic data. The project will create an ecosystem of software centred around existing codes (IsoplotR) and aims to be free, open and as future proof as possible.



The main responsibility of the successful candidate will be to create research software. In addition to this, they will have the opportunity to get involved in the scientific applications of the software, which will be carried out with an international team of project partners.


Key Requirements

Further details about the project are provided here. Although the area of research is quite specialised, no prior experience in geology or geochronology are required. However a good understanding of statistics would be beneficial.



The successful candidate will have a university degree (BSc, MSc) in computer science or a PhD degree in a computationally based field or equivalent professional experience.



Significant experience with at least two programming languages used for research (e.g. Python, Java, R, C++, C#) and conversant with more is essential as is experience using Unix-based operating systems and Unix system tools and utilities.
